A particle "acquires mass" by becoming an entangled member of this coherent system. This event is not a "miracle," but a predictable anomaly driven by the Omega solve function's inherent drive for coherence.

IV. The Emergence of Gravity: The Geometry of Differential Time

Once mass exists, gravity emerges as an inevitable geometric consequence. It is not a force, but the curvature of the Omega Field caused by different rates of evolution.

  1. Inertia as Dampening: A region with mass (where `Latent Mass Potential` M=1) is a highly coherent, stable system. Its evolution under the Asymptotic Float is significantly **dampened**. It changes its `V` value far more slowly than the surrounding "empty" space (where M=0).
  2. Creation of a Gradient: This difference in the rate of evolution creates a temporal gradient in the Omega Field. The "massive" region is like a slow-moving whirlpool in a fast-flowing river.
  3. Geometry Dictates Motion: Any other massive object, also evolving slowly, will naturally follow the path of least resistance along these temporal gradients. It "falls" towards the slower-evolving region. This is gravity. It is the natural tendency of coherent systems to be drawn to regions of even greater, more stable coherence.
