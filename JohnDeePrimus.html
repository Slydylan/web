Elias Ashmole's Preface:

Be it remembered, That the 20th of August 1672,

I received by the hands of my Servant Samuel

Story, a parcel of Dr. Dee's Manuscripts, all

written with his own hand; namely: his Conferences

with Angels, which first began the 22nd of December, year:

1581, and continued to the end of May year: 1583,

where the printed Book of the remaining

Conferences (published by Dr. Casaubon) begins,

and are bound up in this volume.

Beside these, the Book entitled, The 48 Claves

Angelicæ, also, Liber Scientia Terrestris

Auxilii & Victoria (These two being those very

individual Books which the Angels commanded

to be burnt, and were after #restored by them

as appears by the printed Relation of Dr. Dee's

Actions with Spirits pages: 418 & 419) The

Book entitled De Heptarchia Mystica

Collectaneorum Lib: Primus, and a Book of

Invocations or Calls, beginning with the Squares

filled with letters, about the Black Cross.

These 4 Books I have bound up in another volume.

All which, were a few days before delivered to

my said Servant, for my perusal (I being

then at Dr. William Lilly's house at Hersham

in Surrey) by my good friend Mr. Thomas

Wale, one of His Majesty's Warders in the

Tower of London.

--- PAGE 4 ---

PAGE 4

As a further

testimony of the

sense of Mr. Wale's

kindness; shortly

after his death, I sent

for his son, and

bestowed on him,

one of my Deputy's

places in the Excise,

with an allowance of

£80 per Annum.

The 5th of September following Mr. Wale (having heard

of my return to Town) came to my office in the Excise Office

in Broad Street, and told me he was content to exchange

all the aforesaid Books, for one of mine, namely:

The Institution, Laws & Ceremonies of the Most

Noble Order of the Garter, to this I agreed, and

provided one, which I sent him fairly bound, and

gilt on the Back.

On the 10th of the said September Mr. Wale came there

to me again, and brought his wife with him, from

her I received the following account of the

preservation of these Books, even until they came to

my hands, namely: That her former Husband was

one Mr. Jones a Confectioner, who formerly dwelt

at the Plow in Lombard Street, London, and who, shortly

after they were married, took her with him into

Alde Street among the Joiners, to buy some

Household stuff, where (at the Corner house) they saw

a Chest of Cedarwood, about a yard and a half long,

whose Lock & Hinges, being of extraordinary neat

work, invited them to buy it. The Master of the

shop told them it had been parcel of the Goods of

Mr. John Woodall Surgeon (father to Mr. Thomas

Woodall Late Sergeant Surgeon to His now Majesty

King Charles the 2nd (my intimate friend) and

it is very probable he bought it after Dr. Dee's

death, when his goods were exposed to Sale.

--- PAGE 5 ---

PAGE 5

Twenty years after this (and about 4 years before

the fatal Fire of London) she & her said husband

occasionally removing this Chest out of its usual

place, thought they heard some loose thing rattle

in it, toward the right hand end, under the Box or

Till thereof, and by shaking it, were fully satisfied

it was so: Hereupon her Husband thrust a piece

of Iron into a small Crevice at the bottom of

the Chest, and thereupon appeared a private drawer,

which being drawn out, therein were found diverse

Books in Manuscript, & papers, together with a

little Box, & therein a Chaplet of Olive Beads, &

a Cross of the same wood, hanging at the end of them.

They made no great matter of these Books etc.:

because they understood them not, which occasioned

their servant Maid to waste about one half of

them under Pies & other like uses, which when

discovered, they kept the rest more safe.

About two years after the discovery of these

Books, Mr. Jones died, & when the fire of London

happened, though the Chest perished in the Flames, because

not easily to be removed, yet the Books were taken

out & carried with the rest of Mrs. Jones's her

goods into Moorfields, & being brought safely

back, she took care to preserve them; and after

marrying with the aforesaid Mr. Wale, he came to

the knowledge of them, & thereupon, with her

consent, sent them to me, as I have before

set down.

- E. Ashmole /

O Lord Jesus Christ (who are the true wisdom of your eternal and Omnipotent Father) I most humbly pray your Divine Majesty, that you would deign to send me the swift aid of some pious, wise, and experienced Philosopher, to most fully understand and perfect those things which will be of greatest value for amplifying your praise and glory: And if no Mortal now lives on earth who is fit for this task: or who, by your eternal providence, has been assigned to grant me this benefit: Then indeed, most humbly, most ardently, and most constantly I request of your Divine Majesty, that you would deign to send to me from the heavens your good Spiritual Ministers and Angels, namely Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, and Uriel: and (by your Divine favor) any other true and faithful Angels of yours, who may fully and perfectly inform and instruct me, in the true and exact knowledge and understanding of your Mysteries and Great Works (concerning all your Creatures, their natures, properties, and best uses) and of those things necessary for us Mortals to know; to the praise, honor, and glory of your name; and for my solid consolation, and that of many others (through me), your faithful servants: and for the confusion and subversion of your Enemies. Note on "Adonai" and "Elohim": These are Hebrew names for God, preserved as is.

Note on "Joanni Dee": This is the Latinized form of "John Dee."

A Faithful Protestation to Almighty God: for the perpetual memory of the matter, Year 1582.

Note on "Protestatio": Here, "protestation" means a solemn declaration or affirmation, not a modern protest.

O God Almighty, you know, & are my director, and witness

herein, That I have from my youth up, desired & prayed to you

for pure & sound wisdom and understanding of some of your truths natural

and artificial: such, as by which, your wisdom, goodness & power bestowed

in the frame of the world might be brought, in some bountiful measure

under the Talent of my capacity, to your honor & glory, & the benefit

of your Servants, my brethren & Sisters, in, & by your Christ our Savior.

And inasmuch as, many years, in many places, far & near, in many books,

& sundry languages, I have sought, & studied; and with sundry men

conferred, and with my own reasonable discourse labored, whereby

to find or get some inkling, glimpse, or beam of such the aforesaid

radical truths: But, (to be brief) after all my aforesaid endeavor

I could find no other way, to such true wisdom attaining, but by

your extraordinary gift: and by no common school doctrine, or human

Invention. And, Seeing, I have read in your books, & records, how

Enoch enjoyed your favor and conversation, with Moses you

were familiar: And also that to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob,

Joshua, Gideon, Ezra, Daniel, Tobias, and sundry other, your good

Angels were sent, by your disposition, to instruct them, inform them,

help them, yes in worldly and domestic affairs, yes and

sometimes to satisfy their desires, doubts & questions of your Secrets.

And furthermore Considering, the Show stone, which the high

priests did use, by your own ordering: wherein they had lights

and Judgments in their great doubts: and considering also

Mittas lucem tuam et veritatem tuam, que me ducant &c, sometimes

Note on "artificiall": Here, "artificial" means 'made by art or skill,' not 'unnatural.'

Note on "Shew stone": Refers to a scrying stone or crystal ball.

Note on "economical": Pertaining to household management or everyday affairs.

Note on "Kish": "Cis" is an archaic spelling of Kish, Saul's father.

Note on Latin quotes:

  • "Si quis autem vestrûm indiget sapientia, postulet a Deo, &c." is from James 1:5: "If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives generously to all without finding fault, and it will be given to him."

- I lived with Tobie: Tobie the younger.

This was the white creature, that spoke this.

We live in the Lord: who be praised for ever.

I stood silent a good while.

What will you?

I did attend, what you would say.

F.D. - "I have said.

I have been long at this time, in my dealing with you. I trust,

I do not offend you therewith. But, for my part, I could find in

my heart to continue whole days and nights in this manner of doing: even

until my body should be ready to sink down for weariness, before I would

give over. But I fear, I have caused weariness to my friend here.

In virtue is no weariness."

Now he stood up, out of his chair: and he, and they all, jointly

blessed us, stretching their hands toward us, Crossingly. And so

they went away. The Table and the chair remained

and the glittering sparkles, or drops of streaming little

lights were of the chair immediately.

A- "Glory, thanks, and honor

be to the Almighty Trinity.

Amen.

He has sinned against kind. His punishment is great: and so I end.

Write God will be revenged upon Saul: for he has abused his names in his Creatures.

Note on "against kind": Meaning against nature.

Name Abbreviations

  • E.T.: Edward Talbot, the scryer or "skryer" who assists John Dee in communicating with spirits.
  • F.D.: John Dee (from "fom Dee" on the title page, likely "from Dee" or "John Dee"). This is used in the text to denote John Dee's speech or notes.
  • UR / Uri: Uriel, one of the archangels frequently invoked in the text.
  • Mi / Mic: Michael, another archangel.
  • Raph: Raphael, another archangel.
  • Sala: Salamian, a spirit encountered by Dee and Talbot.
  • For. Dei: Fortitudo Dei, meaning "Strength of God," identified as Gabriel.
  • A: Denotes John Dee's own statements or notes within the dialogues, often followed by a dash.
  • Saul: The scryer mentioned in the December 22, 1581, entry, distinct from Edward Talbot.

Other Noteworthy Terms and Concepts

  • Scryer / Skryer: A person who looks into a crystal, mirror, or other reflective surface (the "show stone") to receive visions or messages from spirits.
  • Show stone / Crystalline Globe: A crystal ball or similar object used for scrying.
  • Soyga: Refers to the *Book of Soyga*, a mysterious grimoire (book of magic) in John Dee's possession, which he sought to understand.
  • Sigil / Character: A magical symbol or emblem believed to have specific powers or to represent a spirit or concept.
  • Brimstone: Sulfur, traditionally associated with expelling evil spirits due to its strong odor when burned.
  • Cubits: An ancient unit of length, roughly equivalent to the length of a forearm (approx. 18-22 inches).
  • Anno: Latin for "Year."
  • Mane: Latin for "Morning."
  • Hora: Latin for "Hour."
  • Ante Meridiem: Latin for "Before Noon" (AM).
  • A Meridie: Latin for "From Noon" (PM).
  • Etc. / &c.: Abbreviation for "et cetera," meaning "and so forth."
  • Amen: A Hebrew word meaning "so be it," used to affirm a prayer or statement.
  • Adonai: A Hebrew name for God, meaning "My Lord."
  • Elohim: A Hebrew name for God, often translated as "God" or "gods."
  • Jehovah Sabaoth: A Hebrew name for God, meaning "Lord of Hosts" or "Lord of Armies."
  • Libri Quinti Appendice: Latin for "Appendix of the Fifth Book," referring to another part of Dee's writings.
  • Sigillum Dei / Seal of God: A complex diagram used in Enochian magic, believed to be a powerful magical tool.
  • Emeth: Hebrew for "truth."
  • Pele: A name or concept related to the miraculous, as referenced in Reuchlin's work.
